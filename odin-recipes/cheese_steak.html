Cheese Steak

Description

A cheesesteak is a sandwich featuring thinly sliced beef, typically ribeye, saut√©ed or grilled, and melted cheese, usually on a hoagie or torpedo roll. It's a popular regional sandwich, with its roots in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

Ingredients

  • Thinly sliced ribeye or brisket
  • Salt & pepper
  • Hoagie roll
  • Mayo
  • Onions
  • Olive Oil
  • Provolone Cheese
  • Garlic

Steps

  1. Heat a skillet on medium-high heat with some olive oil.
  2. Add sliced onions into the pan and sweat until brown.
  3. Remove the onions into a separate bowl.
  4. Then evenly distribute the ribeye on the skillet and leave it undisturbed for 4 minutes to develop a flavorful crust.
  5. Flip and cook the rest of the ribeye until brown.
  6. Add garlic and the onions from the bowl with the skillet on medium-low heat.
  7. Place the provolone cheese slices in a line approximately the same length as your hoagie roll.
  8. Slice your hoagie roll and add a layer of mayonaise on both sides.
  9. Once the cheese has melted, place your cheesesteak into the hoagie roll and enjoy.
